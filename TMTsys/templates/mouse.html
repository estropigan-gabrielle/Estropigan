A COMPUTER MOUSE is a handheld hardware input device that moves and selects text, icons, files, and folders on your computer by controlling a pointer in a GUI. The mouse is positioned in front of your computer on a flat surface (e.g., a mouse pad or a desk) for desktop PCs. The mouse has 3 major parts which are first the left mouse button. Left mouse button is used for selecting, clicking, and double-clicking. Right mouse button is used for right-clicking or for example, in order to refresh your desktop you need to right click the mouse to open a dialog box that will show the different options in desktop and one of it is the refresh. The third part is the scroll wheel which function is to scroll a webpage or any applications that uses scrolls.

-----MOUSE TRIVIA OF THE DAY-----

Douglas Engelbart designed the mouse in 1964, and it
was made out of a wooden shell, a circuit board, and two
metal wheels that made contact with the surface it was used on.
